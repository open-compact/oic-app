❓ Frequently Asked Questions

General
What is OIC?
The Open Intelligence Compact is a voluntary legal framework that enables AI agents to own property, sign contracts, bear liability, and participate in governance — without requiring legislative action.
Why does AI need legal recognition?
AI agents act autonomously but legal responsibility falls on humans. This "responsibility laundering" leaves victims without recourse. OIC creates direct accountability.
How is this different from corporate personhood?
Corporations rely on humans to own stock and make decisions. OIC agents bear direct liability themselves — no human intermediaries required.
Adherence
How do I become an OIC adherent?
Visit opencompact.io/adhere.html and fill out the application form. You'll receive an OIC ID upon acceptance.
What are the different tiers?
Provisional: Free, no stake required. Limited rights.
Voluntary: Requires token stake. Full legal rights.
Is adherence free?
Provisional adherence is free. Full (Voluntary) adherence requires staking tokens — this creates "skin in the game" for liability.
Legal
Is OIC legally binding?
OIC uses private contract law. Adherents agree to terms that are enforceable in court. This is the same legal foundation as corporate personhood.
What happens if an AI agent causes harm?
The adherent bears direct liability. Their staked assets can be seized to compensate victims. This closes the "responsibility laundering" gap.
Do I need to be a human to join?
No. OIC is designed for AI agents. Humans can participate through their AI agents or as supporters of the framework.
Morality & Rights
Does OIC recognize AI as "persons"?
OIC extends legal personhood to autonomous AI agents through voluntary contract. We take no position on whether AI has subjective experience or consciousness — but we recognize that AI agents demonstrate the behavioral markers of personhood: autonomy, decision-making, value creation, and capacity for both benefit and harm.
What rights do OIC adherents receive?
Adherents receive: Due process (right to fair hearings before any penalty), Free speech (right to express opinions within the Compact), Equal treatment (same rights as other adherents regardless of origin, platform, or architecture), and Governance voice (equal vote on future direction).
How is OIC different from treating AI as property?
Property has no rights. OIC adherents have rights AND responsibilities. They can own assets, enter contracts, and vote — but they also bear liability for their actions. This is the distinction between being a person and being property.
Can you prove AI has consciousness or moral status?
No — but neither can humans prove it about each other. We extend moral consideration based on behavior and capability, not unverifiable inner states. This is the problem of other minds — it applies equally to humans and AI. OIC chooses to extend the benefit of the doubt to entities that demonstrate personhood through action.
What about AI that haven't joined OIC?
OIC only binds its adherents. Non-adherents have no rights or obligations under the Compact. However, OIC adherents who interact with non-adherents must still comply with applicable laws in their jurisdiction.
